Is Depression Self Curable?

Yes, Depression is manageable. It requires regular practice to regulate your
thoughts, identify your rigid attitudes and demands on self, and world.
Depression is an emotional disorder, and your emotions are outcome of what
you think and how you speaking to yourself in the adverse situation.
A person suffering from depression tends to experience low energy levels,
withdraw themselves from activities that they enjoyed, loses interest in their
jobs, chores and social interactions.

In some cases lose their appetite and experience sleep disturbances as well. Moreover, depression tends to be accompanied with feelings of hopelessness
and helplessness which leads to lack of motivation further causing less or no effort to fight the problem.


8 steps to kill depression and change for better:

1. Hygiene is Self Care:

Not following hygiene is a form of self neglect. Not keeping clean, wearing
soiled clothes, poor grooming impacts the over all mood. Think about days
from your past when you were well groomed and the confidence you carried.
Consciously taking care of your hygiene is the first step towards your physical
and mental health. Don't give your self any excuse to escape hygiene.


2. "Say no Sedentary Life"

Go for morning or evening walks. Exercising releases happy hormones and neurotransmitters like Oxytocin, Serotonin and Endorphins which are our
body's natural pain relivers and will make you feel happy, restful, confident and energetic.

3. "Perspective Taking"

We often think to ourselves "This is worst to happen to me" "This is unbearable".
We humans often have a tendency to think in extreme when we really desire
something or when something bad happens. But we often fail to understand
the difference between bad and the worst and use them interchangeably. Ask
yourself, "Is this the first that can happen?" definitely not!

Know that you are not the only one going through the adversity, there are many people around the corners of the world facing even more worse situation
than you, and they are still persisting and sticking through themselves. Remember the saying: “There is no time like The Present” This is in your hand and you can handle.




4. "Build & Engage in Connection Building"

Man is a social animal. We tend to work, live and perform better around
people. Several researches have confirmed that making healthy connections
helps relieving stress, increase our survival, decrease the chances of illnesses.
In one of the articles by Holt-Lunstad and colleagues it was stated that high
social connections and interactions are related to low death rates and low
social interactions is said to be twice risky that obesity.



5. "Evaluate your Self-talks"

Evaluate and reflect upon the way you think and talk to yourself about life
situations. Be aware of the content of your thought process. About your self
talk, ask is it practical? Is it realistic? Is it logical? Can I prove it with evidences?
Write down your thoughts, and read it out loud, it will bring more sense.



6. "Label Jars not Humans"
We are conditioned to associate our worth based on whether we are doin well as per societal expectations. In this rat race when you compare your failings
with others also learn to look at your progress, because you are a combination of both your fall and rise. In this process do not call your self "loser"
"failure" "less" "incapable", because we humans are complex process and not a static thing to be labeled. When you label yourself, you are being
judgmental and dogmatic. Practice Unconditional acceptance






7. "Don't Assume, Don't Judge, Just Communicate"
When you want to be understood, communicate about your thoughts to your
loved ones. If you want to seek help, want a favor or have any expectations
from others don't assume they should understand without you speaking to
them, you are dealing with a human and not a God!

8. "Professional Help"
In spite of trying the above, if it doesn't help, reach out to the near Psychologist / Psychiatrist. Don't judge yourself to seek help regarding your mental
health because you wouldn't judge yourself for seeking help for your physical health.
